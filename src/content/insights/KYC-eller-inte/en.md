---
title: "GDPR and KYC: design principles that hold up in audit"
category: "Regulation"
excerpt: "Regulatory requirements don't have to conflict. The right architecture makes GDPR and KYC natural partners."
date: "2025-09-18"
---

GDPR and KYC regulations pose seemingly conflicting requirements: on one hand you need to collect and retain data for customer due diligence, on the other you must minimize data collection and delete when the purpose is fulfilled.

In practice, it's possible to design systems that meet both requirements. The key lies in clear purposes, automated deletion, and granular access control. Every data point should have a defined purpose and lifecycle.

By building in privacy-by-design from the start — not as an afterthought — you create a system that holds up in both internal and external review.
